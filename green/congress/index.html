WORK TO DATE

I have scraped, cleaned, and subset U.S. congressional speeches pertaining to environmental and labor issues from 1873-2024 (more on the data and keyword selection on Friday). The main text mining approaches I have experimented with include:

  1. Dynamic Topic Modeling: How does the frequency of topics in environmental-labor congressional speeches change over time? How do the words representing a topic evolve over time?
  2. Term Frequency - Inverse Period Frequency: What words are most distinguishing of environmental-labor speeches in different periods?
  3. Co-occurrence Networks: What words co-occur with labor and/or environmental keywords in a given period? How do word co-occurrences evolve over time?

BROADER WORK

The energy transition is marked by both tremendous urgency and tremendous possibility; the sociotechnical blueprints of our new energy system are not fixed. The rollout (and rollback) of new green industrial policy raises questions about what is at stake for US workers and their environments as the energy transition unfolds–and the strategic position workers hold to advance or contest visions of the green world they quite literally build. I am interested in the social and ecological dilemmas that we must contend with to advance an energy transition that is both green and just–and the enviro-labor organizing strategies capable of achieving this.

Some of the questions that motivate my broader doctoral work:

  • How do key state, corporate, environmental, and labor actors articulate “labor” and “environment” relations in struggles over green onshoring?
  • How do (and don’t) these shifting green supply chains transform geographies of solidarity (1) within the labor movement and (2) between workers and environmentalists?
  • How do working and organizing conditions vary across different nodes of the EV and battery supply chain? How do working and organizing conditions surrounding EV/battery production vary geographically within the U.S. (e.g., proposed battery plants in California vs. Tennessee)?
  • What strategic challenges and opportunities do these shifting geographies of green production pose for mass enviro-labor organizing?
